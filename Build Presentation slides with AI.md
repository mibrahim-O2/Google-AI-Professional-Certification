Build with AI: Presentation slides
Step 1: Build the slide deck
Click the “New chat” button to start a new chat with Gemini. Upload the document that you want to turn into a slide deck. Click the “Plus” icon in the Gemini prompt box, where you can either upload a file, or you can add it from Drive. 

You can use your own document or use a sample document outlining a service overview from an IT business 
here
.

Then, click the Tools bar and select Canvas.

Paste the prompt below into the input bar, editing the bracketed portions to match your needs:

Acting as an expert [type of expertise you need, ex: designer] help me create a text-only slide deck based on [document you have supplied, ex: new refund policy].

Always double check Gemini’s work. Make sure that the information in the slides matches the information in the doc you gave it to ensure AI hasn’t hallucinated or missed anything.

If you notice Gemini hallucinated or missed a key detail from your uploaded document, you can correct Gemini with the following prompt: 

You missed [specific detail] from the attached source document, please add it to [slide number].

Step 2: Edit your slides
You can ask Gemini to make changes to the deck as well, such as adding information or changing the color of the slides, for example:

Update the last slide to say: [edit you want Gemini to make].

Or:

Make the tone of the entire deck more enthusiastic and sales-focused.

Or:

Add a column to the table on Slide 5 that includes [specific data].

Make changes as you need them. 

Step 3: Customize for different audiences
You can use Gemini to adjust the slides for a different audience. Give Gemini details about what would make the slides better for that particular audience. 

Paste the prompt below:

Change the slide deck to be for [new type deck you need, ex: an internal onboarding deck for new hires]. Use only the information provided in the slides.

Again, make sure all the information is correct, and ask Gemini to make any changes you need. 

Once you get it to a place where you are happy with the slides, you can export it to Google Slides, where you can share it with your team and continue editing the slides in a more hands-on manner. You can also download the slides as a PDF. 

Next steps:
Whenever you are faced with a dense document, whether it’s a service offering, policy handbook, or a data-heavy report, think about using this process to transform that passive text into a more engaging slide-deck.

Build with AI: High-impact slide deck
To use the features covered in this lab, sign into Gemini using your Google Account.

As you follow the video instructions in this lab, customize the prompts below to fit your own work.

Feel free to pause the video as needed.

The skills you learn in this lab will help you to increase the quality of your slide deck presentations.

Lab tasks:
Step 1: Edit slide text
In Google Slides, navigate to a slide you want to improve. If you don’t have your own presentation, you can use slide 4 in this 
sample Slides deck
. Click the Gemini icon in the top right corner to open the sidebar. Paste the prompt below and replace the text in [brackets] with your own details.

I’d like to make this slide more readable. Write [number, e.g. three] sets of short headlines and descriptions, that can replace the text on this slide.

Review Gemini's suggestions and replace the text on your slide.

Step 2: Generate images for your key points
In the same Gemini sidebar, you will now generate images for your key points, one at a time. Start by pasting the first prompt below, replacing the text in [brackets] with your own details.

Generate an image to illustrate [a key point from your slide]. Make it [desired image color] and the background [desired background color].

Review the generated images and insert your favorite one onto the slide. To generate more images, paste the following prompt, referencing the option you just selected to maintain a consistent style.

I like the [your preferred option from the previous step, e.g. top-right] option best. Create another illustration in the same style, this time depicting a [subject for the next key point on your slide].

You can repeat this process as many times as you’d like, or you can provide different instructions to generate different images.
